# Pareto AQL Inspection Guide

## Pareto Prioritization

- Validate category definitions before ranking.
- Separate count Pareto from cost, severity, and customer-risk Pareto.
- Stratify by product, line, supplier, lot, inspector, time period, or customer when aggregation hides a cause.
- Use before-after Pareto when evaluating improvements.
- Do not interpret the largest category as root cause; it is only the best starting point.

## AQL And Sampling

Confirm:

1. lot size;
2. inspection level;
3. AQL;
4. defect class such as critical, major, minor;
5. normal, tightened, or reduced inspection;
6. single, double, or multiple sampling;
7. acceptance and rejection numbers;
8. regulatory or customer standard used.

## Risk Interpretation

- Producer risk: good lots rejected.
- Customer risk: bad lots accepted.
- OC curve: probability of accepting a lot at different defect levels.
- AQL is not the promised outgoing quality level.
- Sampling protects decisions; it does not improve the process by itself.

## Reporting

State the standard or package used, assumptions, lot disposition rule, and the practical risk of the selected plan.
